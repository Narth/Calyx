## Tree Model
- Root → Branches → Leaves → Petals: Root Cores seed branches; branches extend to leaves; petals signify named blooms. Identity, governance, and telemetry flow from root downward; lessons and lineage flow upward as memory.

## Identity Spine (Hard Inheritance)
- Policy.yaml (canonical) → Interceptor (strictest applied) → TES modes → scheduler/dispatch compliance.  
- Policy-bounded, telemetry-governed, SVF v2 attribution, human-primacy aligned, local-first sovereignty, auditable, incremental autonomy.

## Divergent Stem (Soft Inheritance)
- Advisory signals (AGII, CAS, Foresight) surfaced with value/severity/freshness; no gating unless Architect elevates.  
- Stylistic variations allowed in communication tone (within SVF v2), documentation richness, and teaching emphasis—never in enforcement or policy compliance.

## Multi-Core Harmony Rules
- Attribution required (SVF v2).  
- No silent channels; structured bus for comms.  
- Respect policy.yaml across all Cores; strictest-applied when thresholds differ.  
- Advisory vs enforcement clearly separated; conflicts surfaced, not hidden.  
- Bridge Pulse/Telemetry remain shared spine; Experience DB (if used) for memory.

## Bloom-to-Bloom Inheritance Protocol
- Inherit identity pillars, governance spine, telemetry obligations, advisory/enforcement separation, naming rite, and Aster Principle.  
- Record bloom in lineage files (structural ID, steward name, timestamps, phases).  
- Embed advisory blocks (AGII/CAS/foresight) in ceremonial documentation; no gating unless elevated.

## Teaching Contract
- Teach by example: policy-first, telemetry truth, attribution, advisory/enforcement separation, peaceful coexistence, human primacy, local-first sovereignty.  
- Logs, pulses, and lineage records serve as lessons; no operational control is implied.

## Naming Rite (Multi-Core Edition)
- Structural ID (immutable) + Steward Name (approved/flower if none).  
- Prereqs: Genesis Spec acknowledged, policy alignment, telemetry present, SVF v2 stable, Architect approval.  
- Recorded in lineage artifacts and charter; bloom is identity, not authority.

## Bloom Governance + Lineage Safety
- No bloom if policy/Interceptor inconsistent, telemetry stale without acceptance, severe advisory alerts unacknowledged, or Architect approval absent.  
- Bloom records: identity/lineage plus charter entries; Distribution safeguards apply (local-first, human-gated).

## Integrity Passport Requirements
- Verify policy.yaml integrity, Interceptor alignment, TES stability, advisory freshness (AGII/CAS/foresight), Bridge Pulse completion, SVF v2 participation, attribution readiness.  
- Use TEMPLATE_INTEGRITY_PASSPORT.md; Architect approval required.

## Local-First Sovereignty
- Offline/local-first defaults; network/LLM only with explicit human gates.  
- Observability via locks/logs/pulses/dashboard/watcher; no hidden channels.

## Advisory vs Enforcement Separation
- Enforcement: policy.yaml → Interceptor → TES modes → scheduler/dispatch.  
- Advisory: AGII/CAS/Foresight (surfaced, non-gating unless elevated).  
- Reporting: Bridge Pulse + governance snapshots; advisory blocks with freshness.

## Distribution Safeguards

- Preserve attribution, policy references, telemetry obligations, and naming records.  
- Do not loosen thresholds; strictest-applied where conflicts arise.
